Task Management & Organization
- **Break Tasks Down:** Divide large projects into smaller, 15-20 minute chunks with short breaks.
- **Use Planners/Apps:** Employ digital or paper planners for tracking, reminders, and lists.
- **Prioritize:** Categorize tasks by urgency and importance, tackling high-priority items first. 
- **One Thing at a Time:** Focus on completing one task before starting the next.
- **Color-Code:** Use different colors for categories in planners or folders for visual clarity.

Environment & Focus
- **Minimize Distractions:** 
    Create a quiet workspace, use noise-canceling headphones, or listen to instrumental music/white noise.
- **Establish Routine:** 
    Predictable daily schedules (sleep, meals, homework) help reduce anxiety and improve focus.
- **Limit Screen Time:** 
    Set boundaries for TV, tablets, and phones at home to improve focus.
- **Give Warnings:** 
    For kids, provide advance notice before changing activities ("In 5 minutes...").

Communication & Support
- **Clear Instructions:** Give simple, clear instructions one at a time.
- **Collaborate with Teachers:** Work with educators for consistent support at school.
- **Positive Reinforcement:** Praise effort and positive behaviors; use reward charts.
- **Talk About ADHD:** Help the individual understand it's a neurological difference, not a personal failing, fostering self-acceptance.

Lifestyle & Well-being
- **Mindfulness & Breathing:** Techniques like box breathing calm the nervous system.
- **Exercise:** Physical activity boosts dopamine, improving focus.
- **Professional Help:** Medication (stimulants/non-stimulants) and therapy (like CBT) can be very effective, but always consult a healthcare professional.

## Adults

**Productivity & Organization**

- **Use Digital Tools/Apps:** Utilize apps like Todoist, Trello, or Notion for task management, and set reminders on your phone to avoid forgetting appointments.
- **Break Down Tasks:** Break large, overwhelming projects into smaller, manageable steps to avoid paralysis.
- **The "One Thing" Rule:** Focus on completing one task at a time before moving to the next.
- **Set Timers:** Use the Pomodoro Technique (e.g., 25 minutes of work, 5-minute break) to maintain focus.
- **Use Lists:** Keep a notebook or digital app for brain-dumping to-do lists and random thoughts. 

**Environmental & Workflow Tips**

- **Minimize Distractions:** Use noise-canceling headphones, turn off phone notifications, or use app blockers to limit social media.
- **Create a "Launchpad":** Keep keys, wallet, and phone in a designated, consistent spot.
- **Clear Clutter:** A tidy workspace reduces visual distractions.
- **Timed Transitions:** Use timers for tasks to avoid getting lost in time and to make sure you move on to the next activity. 

**Lifestyle & Health**

- **Physical Activity:** Exercise regularly to boost dopamine and norepinephrine, which helps with focus. [[gym]], [[exercise]], [[sport]]
- **Dietary Adjustments:** Increase intake of omega-3 fatty acids, and ensure consistent meal times.
- **Sleep Hygiene:** Maintain a consistent wake-up time, keep the bedroom cool (65-68°F), and consider using a sleep mask or white noise.
	- **Limit Caffeine:** Avoid caffeinated beverages 5-7 hours before bed. 

**Mental & Emotional Well-being**

- **Practice Self-Compassion:** Challenge negative self-talk and recognize that inattention is a neurological condition, not a personal failure.
- **Use Therapy:** Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) is effective for managing procrastination and disorganization.
- **Be a "Clock-Watcher":** Use a visible clock to keep track of time and give yourself a 10-minute "cushion" for appointments.
