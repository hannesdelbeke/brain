There’s been concern that lots of intense endurance exercise might increase the risk of heart rhythm problems like atrial fibrillation. Cross-country skiing is a very demanding endurance sport, so researchers wanted to check if regular skiers were at higher risk.

### What they did

Researchers followed a large group of **middle-aged Finnish men** over many years. They looked at:

- How often they did cross-country skiing
- How long and intensely they skied
- Whether they later developed atrial fibrillation or had a stroke

They also adjusted for other health factors like age and lifestyle.

### What they found

**Good news:** Leisure-time cross-country skiing was **not associated with a higher risk** of:

- [[Atrial fibrillation]]
- [[Stroke]]

In simple terms: men who skied regularly did **not** seem more likely to develop these problems than those who didn’t.

### What it means

For most normal people, this suggests that **vigorous endurance exercise like recreational skiing is generally safe**, at least in this group of men.

It also adds to evidence that exercise’s benefits usually outweigh concerns about heart rhythm issues.

### Important limits

This study was only in:

- Middle-aged men
- Mostly white / Finnish population

So results may not apply equally to:

- Women
- Younger people
- Older people
- Different ethnic groups

Also, it was an observational study, so it shows associations, not absolute proof.

## Bottom line

Doing regular hard aerobic exercise like cross-country skiing **did not increase stroke or irregular heartbeat risk** in this study
