Gear used to climb Mera Peak 6.461m
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UhGDKZoD6eA

- **Scarpa Phantom 8000**: 600 USD, superb boot, but overkill for 6.000m mountains
- **Icebreaker Merino Baselayer**: 110€ & 100€, costly, but really warm and good quality! Highly recommend
- **Mammut Advanced Hooded Jacket**: 180€, expensive, but the best fleece I've ever owned
- **Schöffel Miara Softshell**: 140€, good softshell jacket, very good price/quality
- **Mountain Equipment Kryos Jacket**: 300€, bought in on sale, very light and warm, expensive if not on sale (625€)
- **Rab Argon** Down Pants: 180€, very warm down pants, overkill for Mera Peak, good for 7000m
- **Icebreaker Merino Glove Liners**: 25€, warm, but rip very easily on contact with crampons/velcro, be careful
- **Hestra Heli Ski Glove**: 130€, very sturdy and very warm finger gloves, highly recommended!
- **Mountain Equipment Redline Mittens**: Warm, but there are better models (Black Diamond Absolute Mitts)
- **Gregory Alpinisto 35L backpack**: 120€, versatile backpack for anything in the mountains, very good
- **Petzl Actik Core Headlamp**: 60€, my go-to headlamp in the mountains, light, versatile, easy to use with gloves
- **Black Diamond Trail Poles**: 90€, good poles, recommend Aluminium Poles as they don't break so easily
- **Sunglasses: Any Category 3** (very sunny) or Category 4 (glacier) you can afford, Julbo is very high quality
- **KLEAN Kanteen TKPro Bottle**: Very good insulted bottle, only steel and silicon, keeps drinks really hot!
