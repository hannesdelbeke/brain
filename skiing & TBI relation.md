## Conclusion
Winter sports are generally worth doing, but skiing, snowboarding, and hockey have meaningful [[concussion]] risk because of speed + hard impacts. Use [[helmet|helmets]], ski within your limits, and treat any head injury seriously.

## Plain English Summary

### What is TBI?

A **traumatic brain injury** happens when a blow, jolt, or sudden movement damages the brain. It ranges from:
- **Mild** = concussion
- **Moderate/severe** = bleeding, swelling, or more serious brain damage
    

The article focuses mostly on sports-related concussions. ([Neurology Advisor](https://www.neurologyadvisor.com/subjects/neurotrauma/?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Neurotrauma Archives - Neurology Advisor"))

## Why winter sports can be risky

Winter sports often combine:

- **Speed**
- **Hard surfaces** (ice, packed snow)
- **Falls**
- **Collisions with people, trees, barriers**
- **Unpredictable terrain**

That means a crash while skiing or snowboarding can create strong forces on the head and neck. ([wxpr.org](https://www.wxpr.org/health/2026-02-06/thrill-of-winter-sports-can-also-raise-the-risk-of-concussions?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Thrill of winter sports can also raise the risk of concussions | WXPR"))

## Which sports have higher risk?

The article highlights:

- **Downhill skiing**
- [[Snowboarding]]
- **Ice hockey**
- Sometimes skating or sledding accidents too

Snowboarding is often riskier for beginners because people fall backwards or catch edges suddenly. ([weillcornell.org](https://weillcornell.org/news/national-winter-sports-traumatic-brain-injury-tbi-awareness?utm_source=chatgpt.com "National Winter Sports Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) Awareness | Patient Care"))
## Signs of concussion
Symptoms may appear right away or later:
- [[Headache]]
- Dizziness
- Nausea
- [[Confusion]]
- Memory problems
- Sensitivity to light/noise
- Balance problems
- Feeling “foggy”

Important: you do **not** need to be knocked out to have a concussion. ([weillcornell.org](https://weillcornell.org/news/national-winter-sports-traumatic-brain-injury-tbi-awareness?utm_source=chatgpt.com "National Winter Sports Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI) Awareness | Patient Care"))

## How to reduce risk

The article recommends:
- Wear a **properly fitted [[helmet]]**
- Stay within your skill level
- Learn technique / take lessons
- Control speed
- Avoid [[alcohol]] or [[fatigue]] while skiing
- Respect weather and slope conditions
- Stop immediately after a head injury

Helmets help reduce some injuries, though they do not prevent every concussion. ([Brain Injury Association of America](https://biausa.org/public-affairs/media/winter-sports-safety?utm_source=chatgpt.com "Winter Sports Safety 101: Understanding the Impact of Concussions and How to Stay Protected - Brain Injury Association of America"))

## If you hit your head

Do **not** “shake it off” and continue.

You should stop activity and seek medical advice if symptoms appear, because repeated injury before recovery can be dangerous.
